Written by Luna in 2025 for a Proslav poetry contest.

In a land far away, right now and back then, Where resolute millions, Fight to be free, The Palestinian sun will shine again, From desert to mountain, From river to sea.

No settler, no murder, no cowardly thief, Stuck to their chiefdom, In all certainty, Know the inspired native's strong belief, In peace and in freedom, In true liberty.

For 80 years, the struggle went on, Martyr for martyr, Man for man, They never left each other alone, Fighter for fighter, The fray began.

No 'Merican soldiers, no murders of kids, No British soldiers, No orange men, Could cross Palestine without a miss, Its Judaean boulders, Or Gaza's defense.

The world may be watching or cover its eyes, At war and at poverty, At genocide, But the struggle of Palestine never dies! For freedom and liberty, Which shall abide.

In a land far away, right now and back then, Where resolute millions, Fight to be free, The Palestinian sun will shine again, From desert to mountain, From river to sea.
